## **UNDERSTANDING ELDORIAN CALENDARS**

### **The Common Reckoning (CR)**

**Year 0**: The signing of the Treaty of Two Paths  
**Current Year**: 200 CR

The Common Reckoning is the most widely used dating system in Eldoria, adopted by most nations for trade, diplomacy, and international communication. Events before Year 0 are marked as "BT" (Before Treaty).

**Usage**: Official documents, trade agreements, international treaties, historical texts meant for general audiences.

### **The Veinborn Reckoning (VR)**

**Year 0**: The establishment of the Vein Compact  
**Current Year**: 150 VR

Used primarily by magical academies, research institutions, and Veinborn organizations. Scholars often include both CR and VR dates in their writings.

**Usage**: Academic papers, magical research, Veinborn guild records, enchanter certifications.

### **Regional Calendar Systems**

**Karothas War Calendar (KWC)** \- \*Drakkaros

* Marks years by military campaigns and the reigns of Warlord-Kings. The current Warlord-King began their reign 37 years ago, so the year is "37th Year of Warlord-King Draegon."

**Elarion Natural Cycle (ENC)** \- \*Elarion

* No numbered years. Events measured by natural phenomena: "the third great blooming," "the year of double moons," "the season when stars fell like rain." Elarion natives find this perfectly clear; outsiders find it maddeningly vague.

**Kulois Shadow Count (KSC)** \- \*Kulois

* Based on astronomical cycles visible in perpetual twilight. Current cycle: "The 47th Umbral Convergence." Each convergence lasts approximately 4.3 standard years, making conversion difficult.

**Zepharyth Storm Cycles (ZSC)** \- \*Zepharyth

* Measures time by the rotation period of The Maelstrom (the permanent hurricane system). One full rotation \= approximately 7.2 standard years. Currently in the "23rd Storm Cycle."

**Ordanthia Artifact Epochs (OAE)** \- \*Ordanthia

* Events dated by major archaeological discoveries. Current epoch: "The Age of the Silent City" (began 18 years ago when the city was discovered). Previous epoch: "The Age of the Screaming Vaults" (lasted 62 years).

**Glacoria Survival Count (GSC)** \- \*Glacoria

* Simple generational counting. Current generation: "The 14th Cold Generation" (since unified Clargon Enclave formed). Each generation \= approximately 15 years, marked by the coming-of-age ceremony of the Iceborn.

## **THE TITAN AGE (2,000 \- 1,500 BT )**

### **Early Titan Age (2,000 \- 1,800 BT)**

**\~2,000 BT** | *Before any current calendar systems*

* **The Titanforged Ascendancy**: The Titanforged civilization reaches its apex in Ordanthia

* Society built on massive scale---cities with buildings for beings three times human height

* Master ambient runic crafting; create self-sustaining magical infrastructure

* Begin intensive Mana Vein manipulation to power their expanding empire

* **Cultural Notes**: This period exists in legend across all modern cultures. Artifacts from this era are priceless.

**\~1,950 BT**

* Titanforged expand beyond Ordanthia, establishing outposts on other continents

* First recorded creation of ambient-runed weapons and armor

* Development of the geometric principles that modern runesmiths still study (and mostly fail to replicate)

**\~1,900 BT**

* **Peak of Titanforged Power**: Civilization controls major Mana Veins on three continents

* Floating cities, self-repairing structures, weapons that never dull

* Population reaches estimated 50 million (across all Titanforged territories)

**\~1,850 BT**

* Titanforged push limits of Mana Vein exploitation

* First warnings from dissenting scholars (records fragmentary, found in ruins)

* Minor Veinstorms begin occurring more frequently in Titanforged territories

**\~1,800 BT**

* **The Syr'kai Golden Age**: Ocean civilization flourishes beneath the waves

* Syr'kai control underwater Mana Veins, build magnificent aquatic cities

* Trade relationships with surface races, including Titanforged

* Syr'kai population estimated at 30 million across oceanic territories

### **The Cataclysm Period (1,800 \- 1,500 BT)**

**\~1,750 BT**

* First major Veinstorm strikes Ordanthia, destroys a Titanforged city

* Titanforged leadership ignores warnings, intensifies Vein exploitation to rebuild

* Syr'kai express concern over instability in underwater Veins

**\~1,700 BT**

* Multiple Veinstorms ravage Titanforged territories

* Titanforged scholars desperately attempt to stabilize the Vein network

* Their efforts paradoxically make the problem worse---like trying to stop a flood by digging the riverbed deeper

**\~1,650 BT**

* **The First Blighted Zone**: A collapsed Mana Vein creates permanent magical corruption

* Titanforged evacuate surrounding territories but refuse to abandon major cities

* Internal civil conflict---some Titanforged want to flee, others insist they can fix the problem

**\~1,600 BT**

* Cascading Vein failures spread to underwater network

* Syr'kai cities begin experiencing Veinstorms

* Surface-dwelling races mostly unaffected (their minimal Vein exploitation kept them safe)

**\~1,550 BT**

* **The Great Sundering**: A series of catastrophic Veinstorms over 50 years

* Titanforged capital consumed by what would become The Scar

* Underwater Veinstorms collapse Syr'kai cities into ocean trenches

* Estimated combined death toll: 60+ million

**\~1,500 BT**

* **The Cataclysm Ends**: Both civilizations effectively extinct

* Scattered Titanforged survivors exist but cannot maintain their civilization

* Syr'kai survivors retreat to deepest oceans---occasional rumors suggest some still live there

* Global climate disruption from Vein instability affects all continents

* Surface races enter a dark age as trade networks collapse and climate shifts

## **THE DARK CENTURIES (1,500 \- 1,000 BT / \~3,000 \- 2,500 years before present)**

### **The Collapse (1,500 \- 1,300 BT)**

**\~1,500 \- 1,400 BT** | *"The Ash Years" in oral traditions*

* Global crop failures from climate disruption

* Populations crash across all continents (estimated 60% mortality among surface races)

* Knowledge loss---written records destroyed, scholars die, oral traditions fracture

* Survivors focus on basic survival; grand civilization becomes irrelevant

**\~1,400 \- 1,300 BT** | *"The Wandering Time"*

* Massive migrations as populations flee uninhabitable regions

* Blighted Zones slowly expand from Cataclysm sites

* Tribal warfare over remaining fertile land

* Magic viewed with suspicion and fear---Veinborn often killed or exiled

### **The Rebuilding (1,300 \- 1,000 BT)**

**\~1,300 BT**

* Climate begins stabilizing as Mana Vein network settles into new equilibrium

* Small settlements grow into villages

* First post-Cataclysm writing systems developed (independently on different continents)

**\~1,200 BT** | *"The Rediscovery"*

* Explorers begin entering Titanforged ruins (The ruins in Ordanthia)

* First ambient-runed artifacts recovered

* Scholars attempt to understand Titanforged techniques---mostly fail

* Veinborn slowly regain social acceptance as generations forget the Cataclysm's cause

**\~1,150 BT**

* **Development of Powered Runes**: Unable to replicate ambient runes, enchanters discover alternative approach

* Powered runes require active channeling but are far easier to create

* This breakthrough democratizes magic---enchanted items become available to more than just the wealthy elite

**\~1,100 BT**

* Kingdoms emerge from successful city-states

* First recognizable precursors to modern nations form

* Competition begins for control of accessible Mana Veins

* Archaeological expeditions to ruins become organized, funded by kingdoms seeking lost knowledge

**\~1,050 BT**

* Trade networks re-establish between continents

* Population recovery continues---world population reaches estimated 100 million

* First magical academies founded to formalize Veinborn training

**\~1,000 BT** | **The War of the Veins Begins**

* No single triggering event---gradual escalation of border conflicts over Vein access

* Multiple kingdoms claim the same Major Veins

* Small skirmishes grow into sustained campaigns

* Future historians will mark this period as when "the Thousand-Year War" began

**Calendar Note**: This is 1,200 years before the Treaty of Two Paths (0 CR), meaning it's 1,000 BT in Common Reckoning.

## **MAJOR HISTORICAL EVENTS \- DETAILED ACCOUNTS**

# EVENT 1: THE CATACLYSM (\~1,800-1,500 BT)

## **Quick Summary**

The Cataclysm was a cascading failure of the global Mana Vein network spanning 300 years, culminating in 50 years of apocalyptic Veinstorms. The Titanforged and Syr'kai civilizations—having exploited Veins beyond sustainable limits—were destroyed. Combined death toll: 60+ million. Surface races lost 60% of their population. Global civilization collapsed, entering the Dark Centuries.

**Legacy**: Eldoria's ultimate cautionary tale about hubris and environmental destruction.

## 

## **Detailed Account**

### **Phase 1: The Warning Years (1,850-1,750 BT)**

**Early Signs of Instability:**

* Minor Veinstorms occurring monthly instead of annually  
* Mana Vein radiation climbing steadily  
* Wildlife fleeing regions near Major Veins  
* Ambient runes flickering unpredictably  
* Strange auroras visible even during daylight

**The Dissenting Scholars:**

A faction of Titanforged researchers called "The Harbingers" began warning of systemic collapse:

**Key Figures:**

* **Constructor Veyn** \- Calculated Vein network operating at 140% of sustainable capacity  
* **Archive-Keeper Null** \- Found records of previous collapses in ancient civilizations  
* **Sensor Krev** \- Predicted cascading failures within 50-100 years

**Their Warning (1,840 BT):**

**The Rejection:**

Titanforged leadership dismissed warnings:

* Economic dependence on abundant magical power  
* Arrogance—belief they could engineer solutions  
* Political pressure—reducing exploitation would cause economic collapse  
* Incomplete understanding—their "solutions" made things worse  
* Success bias—centuries of supremacy bred overconfidence

The Harbingers were marginalized, exiled, or silenced. Constructor Veyn's research was buried—only rediscovered 2,000 years later.

**Syr'kai Response:**

Underwater civilization more attuned to environmental changes:

**1,820 BT**: Syr'kai warned surface races of instability    
**1,810 BT**: Began evacuating shallow cities    
**1,800 BT**: Initiated "Great Withdrawal"—reduced Vein exploitation 40%

**Tragic Irony**: Syr'kai's responsible action came too late. Titanforged exploitation continued, and underwater Veins were too interconnected with land networks to stabilize independently.

### **Phase 2: The Cascade Begins (1,750-1,650 BT)**

**The First Major Veinstorm (1,750 BT):**

**Location**: Titanforged city Krev'sharn (population 200,000)

**What Happened:**

* Major Vein beneath city collapsed during routine extraction  
* Three-day Veinstorm followed  
* Reality destabilization—gravity fluctuations, time distortions  
* 47,000 dead, 120,000 evacuated  
* City center became first documented Blighted Zone

## **Terms for MUNDANES**

### **Neutral/Respectful Synonyms**

* **Steelborn** \- Emphasizes their reliance on physical prowess and crafted weapons rather than magic  
* **The Grounded** \- Refers to their connection to practical, physical reality  
* **Ironbloods** \- Honorific term highlighting their physical resilience and "unmagical" blood

### **Slang (Context-Dependent)**

* **Blanks** \- Common slang referring to their "blank" connection to mana; can be neutral or mildly derogatory depending on tone  
* **Nulls** \- Similar to Blanks but more clinical; often used in magical academic settings  
* **Deads** \- Short for "dead to the Veins"; casual term used among Veinborn, sometimes affectionate in mixed groups

### **Insults/Slurs (Offensive)**

* **Duds** \- Cruel term implying they're "defective" or "failed" humans who didn't develop the Veinborn mutation  
* **Severed** \- Derogatory term suggesting they're "cut off" from the natural flow of magic, implying incompleteness  
* **Hollows** \- Offensive slur meaning "empty inside," implying they lack something essential  
* **Cinders** \- Particularly cruel insult meaning "already burned out" or suggesting they're the ashes of what a person should be

### **Additional Context Terms**

* **Bladesman/Bladesworn** \- Respectful military term for Mundane warriors  
* **The Forged** \- Term emphasizing that Mundanes forge their own strength through effort  
* **Earthbound** \- Can be neutral or slightly condescending depending on speaker

## **Terms for VEINBORN**

### **Neutral/Respectful Synonyms**

* **Conduits** \- Technical/respectful term acknowledging their biological function  
* **The Touched** \- Neutral term meaning "touched by the Veins"; implies blessing or curse depending on context  
* **Channelers** \- Formal, respectful term used in academic or professional settings

### **Slang (Context-Dependent)**

* **Sparks** \- Common casual term, especially for young or inexperienced Veinborn; can be affectionate or dismissive  
* **Wires** \- Slang emphasizing their role as magical conductors; often used by Mundanes in mixed military units  
* **Glowbloods** \- Refers to how their veins literally glow when channeling; neutral but informal

### **Insults/Slurs (Offensive)**

* **Burnouts** \- Cruel term implying they're destined to self-destruct; particularly offensive to those who've suffered Veinburn  
* **Freaks** \- Generic derogatory term suggesting their mutation makes them unnatural or monstrous  
* **Leakers** \- Offensive slang referring to radiation leakage and Wild Magic accidents; implies instability and danger  
* **Ticking-timers** \- Dehumanizing slur emphasizing that Veinborn are "bombs waiting to explode" from Veinburn

### **Additional Context Terms**

* **Archmage/Battlemage/Pyromancer** etc. \- Respectful professional titles based on specialization  
* **The Radiant** \- Poetic/reverent term used in some cultures that view magic as divine  
* **The Burdened** \- Sympathetic term acknowledging the cost of their power

## **Regional Variations**

### **Karothas (Martial Culture)**

* Mundanes: "Ironkin" (respectful), "Unlit" (neutral)  
* Veinborn: "Flamebloods" (respectful for fire mages), "Volatiles" (concerned/cautious term)

### **Elarion (Nature-Focused)**

* Mundanes: "Rootbound" (respectful), "Stonehands" (neutral)  
* Veinborn: "Veinweavers" (respectful), "Stormbringers" (cautious/fearful)

### **Kulois (Necromantic Nation)**

* Mundanes: "The Living Steel" (respectful), "Fleshbound" (neutral/medical)  
* Veinborn: "Deathweavers" (respectful for necromancers), "Soulburners" (dramatic term)

### **Academic/Military Jargon**

* Mundanes: "Non-conductives" (clinical), "Physical specialists" (formal)  
* Veinborn: "Thaumic operators" (formal), "Radiation channelers" (clinical), "Class-A conductives" (military classification)

## **Social Dynamics**

### **Among Mixed Groups:**

* **Professional settings**: Formal terms (Mundanes, Veinborn, Conduits, etc.)  
* **Friendly banter**: Mild slang (Blanks, Sparks, Wires) used affectionately  
* **Tension/conflict**: Slurs emerge (Duds, Hollows, Burnouts, Freaks)

### **Self-Identification:**

* Many Mundanes have reclaimed terms like "Blank" or "Null" as badges of pride  
* Some Veinborn use "Burnout" ironically among themselves as dark humor about their mortality  
* Mixed-heritage individuals may use both sets of terms depending on context

### **Taboo Terms:**

The most offensive terms (Cinders, Ticking-timers, Hollows) are considered deeply insulting and can provoke violence. Using them in public is socially unacceptable in most civilized areas and may even be legally actionable in some jurisdictions.

## **Historical Evolution**

* **Pre-Treaty Era**: Terms were more segregated and hostile ("Lessers" for Mundanes, "Cursed" for Veinborn)  
* **Post-Treaty of Two Paths**: Formal terminology became standardized (Mundanes, Veinborn)  
* **Modern Era**: Slang has evolved to reflect complex relationships—some reclaimed, some still hurtful  
* **War Zones**: Harsher terms resurface during conflicts; peacetime sees more respectful language

## **Veinborn Rank Hierarchy**

Formal titles based on training level:

\- **Apprentice** \- First Circle training (learning safety and basic control)  
\- **Adept** \- Second Circle training (specialized in chosen discipline)  
\- **Mage** \- Third Circle complete (combat-ready, field-tested)  
\- **Master** \- Passed Master's Trials (can teach, conduct independent research)  
\- **Grandmaster** \- Legendary status (rare, exceptional achievement)

Discipline Prefixes: Combined with specialization to create formal titles  
\- **Pyromancer**, **Cryomancer**, **Geomancer** (Elemental disciplines)  
\- **Necromancer**, **Blood Mage**, **Flesh Shaper** (Kulois disciplines)  
\- **Battlemage**, **War Mage** (Military specialists)  
\- **Archmage** (General high-level title)  
\- **Healer**, **Enchanter**, **Runesmith** (Non-combat specialists)

Examples:  
\- "Master Pyromancer Kael" → Master-rank fire specialist  
\- "Adept Necromancer Vex" → Second Circle death magic student  
\- "Grandmaster Kael" → Legendary status (dropped discipline prefix)
